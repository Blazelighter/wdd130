Words to Know

Standard words used in basic communication

Buff/Nerf

A buff is used to refer to when a character receives a strengthened attribute with a newer version(patch) of the game. A nerf is when an attribute is weakened. These are generally changes made by the game's developers in order to try and balance the game.

Tank

Tanking refers to when a player purposefully takes damage. It's also used to refer to classes whose main purpose is to draw away enemy attacks from their allies and instead towards themselves.

Aggro

Short for aggression, aggro is usually used in reference to how an enemy AI determines who to attack.

Hit/Hurt Boxes

In games a hit box refers to the area in which an attack will connect with another character and deal damage. A hurt box is the part of a character which can be hit with such an attack. These are traditionally visualized using boxes, but other shapes can be used as well.

HP

Hit Points. This refers to the amount of damage a character or object can take before becoming incapacitated and/or killed. For more information on the origin of the term and how it is applied today, I recommend checking out this video.

DPS

Damage per Second: How quickly something or someone can deal out damage to foes. It is also used to refer to classes whose main role is to damage enemies.

GG

Good Game. Used after a match to indicate that you had a good time or as good sportsmanship.

DLC

Downloadable Content: This refers to content released separately from the main game which can be downloaded by the player, sometimes with an additional cost. This can vary from simple aesthetic changes like emotes and costumes (widely referred to as skins), to full expansions that can rival the size of the entire base game.

FPS

Frames per second. This is how many times the computer updates the display. Generally, the more frames per second, the smoother the gameplay looks and feels.
